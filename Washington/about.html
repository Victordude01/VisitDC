Facts about Washington DC

  • According the the 2020 Census, Washington DC has a population of 689,545. Giving it a population larger than that of two U.S. states: Wyoming and Vermont.
  • The three branches of the U.S. federal government are centered in the district: Congress (legislative), the president (executive), and the Supreme Court (judicial).
  • The city was named for George Washington, a Founding Father and the first president of the United States, and the federal district is named after Columbia, a female personification of the nation.
  • Washington DC become the nation's captial on July 16 , 1790 with The Residence Act
